1. Executive Summary
This report evaluates a first-floor residential prototype through Space
Syntax metrics to assess movement and privacy. The results show a
centralized layout where corridors serve as high-integration spines while
private units remain appropriately segregated.

2. Methodology
The floor plan was analyzed using a uniform grid and a "Straighten Wire"
optimization to refine movement paths. This process reduced the path
length by 16.4%, confirming that the corridor geometry supports highly
efficient circulation.

3. Integration (Closeness Centrality)
Highest integration values cluster at the T-shaped corridor junction,
establishing it as the primary distribution hub for the entire floor.
Private rooms remain in the low-integration "blue" zones, ensuring the
acoustic buffering and seclusion necessary for residential living.

4. Choice (Betweenness Centrality)
Betweenness metrics identify the central threshold and right-side corridor
as the most critical bridges for through-movement. These segments carry
the highest traffic load and serve as the most direct routes for daily
navigation and emergency egress.

5. Potential Weak Points
The high integration of the main corridor may feel institutional if
apartment doors open directly onto the busiest zones without a buffer.
Additionally, the extreme segregation of corner units might lead to
social isolation despite offering maximum quietness.

6. Recommendations
Adding recessed entryways for each apartment would create a necessary
transitional threshold between the public corridor and private space. For
the isolated corner units, maintaining their private status is ideal, but
they could benefit from secondary light or air access to offset their
topological depth.

7. Conclusion
The simulation confirms a functionally coherent plan that successfully
balances a high-efficiency spine with private residential pockets. While
the layout is mathematically sound for wayfinding, minor architectural
refinements would better soften the transitions between social and
private realms.
